Regular Verbs
add -ed
Most verbs follow this rule!
walk → walked  ·  play → played
walk → walked
to move on foot
I walked to school yesterday.
She walked home alone last night.
They walked along the beach.
play → played
to take part in a game or activity
He played football yesterday.
They played games all afternoon.
Sara played the piano at the concert.
watch → watched
to look at something carefully
I watched a movie last night.
Tom watched the game on TV.
We watched the birds in the garden.
➕
Verb ends in -e
just add -d
Already has the e — no need to add another!
like → liked  ·  dance → danced
like → liked
to enjoy or feel positive about something
I liked the new restaurant.
She liked the movie very much.
They liked the pizza a lot.
dance → danced
to move your body to music
Sara danced at the party last night.
We danced together all evening.
He danced on the stage beautifully.
smile → smiled
to make a happy expression
She smiled when she got the gift.
Tom smiled and said hello.
They all smiled for the photo.
🔄
Consonant + y
→ drop y, add ied
The y changes to ied!
study → studied  ·  cry → cried
study → studied
to learn about a subject
I studied all night for the test.
Lisa studied English every day.
They studied together at the library.
cry → cried
to have tears from eyes, often when sad
She cried at the sad movie.
He cried when he lost his toy.
The baby cried all night.
try → tried
to attempt or make an effort
I tried the new food and loved it.
He tried very hard to win.
We tried to fix the problem.
🔀
Irregular Verbs
Form changes!
No rule — you must memorise these! 🧠
go → went  ·  eat → ate  ·  see → saw
be → was / were
to exist or describe a state  ·  I/he/she/it → was   you/we/they → were
I was very happy yesterday.
We were at the park all morning.
He was the best player on the team.
go→went
to move to a place
Sara went to the market yesterday.
They went to the beach on Saturday.
I went home right after school.
eat→ate
to put food in your mouth
He ate a big bowl of rice for lunch.
She ate ice cream after dinner.
We ate together as a family.
have→had
to own or experience something
I had a great time at the party.
Tom had a dog when he was young.
They had a test on Monday.
see→saw
to notice with your eyes
I saw a rainbow after the rain.
She saw her friend at the mall.
We saw a funny show last night.
do→did
to perform an action or task
I did my homework right after school.
Lisa did a great job on the project.
They did all the exercises in the book.
come→came
to move toward a place or person
He came to my house last Saturday.
Sara came early to help set up.
We came home before dark.
get→got
to receive or obtain something
I got a new bike for my birthday.
She got a perfect score on the test.
They got lost in the forest.
give→gave
to hand something to someone
He gave her a beautiful flower.
I gave my friend some chocolate.
We gave food to the poor family.
take→took
to carry or move something with you
She took her umbrella because it was raining.
Tom took the bus to work.
They took lots of photos on the trip.
make→made
to create or produce something
I made a card for my mum.
Sara made cookies for the class.
We made a sandcastle at the beach.
know→knew
to have information or understanding
I knew the answer right away.
Tom knew how to fix the problem.
They knew each other for years.
think→thought
to use your mind to form ideas
I thought about the problem all day.
She thought it was a great idea.
We thought the film was boring.
buy→bought
to get something by paying money
He bought a new phone last week.
I bought a gift for my friend.
They bought snacks for the trip.
write→wrote
to put words on paper or screen
I wrote a letter to my grandma.
Lisa wrote a story about dragons.
They wrote their names on the board.
run→ran
to move fast on your feet
I ran 5 km this morning.
He ran to catch the bus.
They ran around the track three times.
fly→flew
to move through the air
She flew to Japan last summer.
A bird flew past my window.
We flew there in two hours.
sing→sang
to make music with your voice  ·  vowel i → a
Sara sang a beautiful song.
I sang along to all the songs.
They sang happy birthday together.
drink→drank
to swallow liquid  ·  vowel i → a
I drank two glasses of water.
Tom drank all of his juice at breakfast.
We drank hot chocolate in the cold.
teach→taught
to help someone learn  ·  -aught pattern
Tom taught us how to cook.
She taught English for ten years.
My dad taught me to ride a bike.
