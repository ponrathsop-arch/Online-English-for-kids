We use it for...

I / You / We / They
was / were + verb-ing
I was sleeping at 9 pm.
You were watching TV.
We were playing in the rain.
They were eating dinner at 7.
He / She / It
was + verb-ing
He was reading a book.
She was cooking dinner.
She was studying all night.
It was raining all day.
was vs were
WAS

WERE

⚡ Interrupted Action
was/were + -ing ... when + past simple
I was sleeping when the phone rang.
She was cooking when the fire alarm went off.
We were playing outside when it started to rain.
He was walking to school when he saw his friend.
🔀 Two Actions at Once
was/were + -ing ... while + was/were + -ing
He was watching TV while she was cooking.
I was studying while my brother was playing games.
The dog was sleeping while the kids were running around.
Sara was singing while Tom was dancing.
Rule 1: Most verbs → add -ing
was / were + verb-ing
Base Verb+ ing
eat→eating
play→playing
watch→watching
Rule 2: verb ends in -e → drop e + ing
Drop the silent e!
Base Verb+ ing
make→making
write→writing
dance→dancing
Rule 3: short vowel + consonant → double it!
Double the last letter!
Base Verb+ ing
run→running
swim→swimming
sit→sitting
get→getting
