Grammar

Could / Couldn't

Talking about past ability — what you could do! 💪

💡 Quick rule: Could is the same for everyone — I, you, he, she, it, we, they all use could! e.g. "I could swim." / "She could sing." / "They could run fast."

🤔 What's the difference?

CAN / CAN'T
Right now — present ability
✅ I can swim. NOW
❌ She can't drive. NOW
❓ Can you sing? NOW
⟷
COULD / COULDN'T
In the past — past ability
✅ I could swim. PAST
❌ She couldn't drive. PAST
❓ Could you sing? PAST
💬 Think of it this way: CAN = what you can do today. COULD = what you could do before!
e.g. "When I was 3, I couldn't read — but now I can!"
