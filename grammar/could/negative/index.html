The Formula
Subject
+
couldn't
+
base verb
couldn't = could not  |  both are correct!  |  same for everyone!
I couldn't swim. = I could not swim.
She couldn't drive. = She could not drive.
He couldn't read. = He could not read.
Where does COULDN'T come from?
could
+
not
→
couldn't
The o in not gets replaced with an apostrophe '
LONG FORM
I could not swim
She could not drive
They could not fly
SHORT FORM ✂️
I couldn't swim
She couldn't drive
They couldn't fly
💡 Both mean exactly the same thing! Couldn't is just faster to say and write.
Verb after COULDN'T
couldn't
+
base verb
(no -s, no -ing, no -ed)
✅ Correct
I couldn't swim
She couldn't drive
He couldn't read
They couldn't talk
❌ Wrong
I couldn't swimming
She couldn't drives
He couldn't reading
They couldn't talked
